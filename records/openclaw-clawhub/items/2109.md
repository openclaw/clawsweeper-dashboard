## Summary

Keep open. The reported gap is still present on current main: package static-scan findings exist and are available through the package version API, but `package moderation-status` and human `package inspect` do not surface rule/file/line details; this is also a ClawHub issue, and repo policy says issues stay open for maintainer follow-up.

## What This Changes

Expose package static-scan rule and file:line details in `package moderation-status` and `package inspect`, document server-side rule IDs, and identify the reporter’s current scanner finding.

## Best Possible Solution

Define a scanner disclosure contract, then expose sanitized staticScan reason codes and rule/file/line findings consistently for authorized package owners and staff in `package moderation-status`, human `package inspect`, and docs.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source inspection shows `moderation-status` and human `package inspect` omit finding details, while the live version endpoint for the reporter’s package returns the exact `suspicious.install_untrusted_source` finding at `openclaw.plugin.json:28`.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. Surfacing sanitized rule/file/line detail is likely the right direction, but maintainers should first decide the disclosure boundary for owner, staff, and public/API access.
