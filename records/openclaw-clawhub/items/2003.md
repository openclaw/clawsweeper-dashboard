## Summary

Keep open: ClawHub cleanup policy keeps issues open, and source inspection confirms a real security-scanner tuning question that needs maintainer/security approval before changing moderation behavior.

## What This Changes

The issue asks for the static moderation scanner to stop over-classifying docker-compose based skills as `suspicious.install_untrusted_source`, or to classify container-image install risk more precisely.

## Best Possible Solution

Refine the manifest install-source heuristic to exempt clearly local/loopback docker-compose endpoints while preserving public raw-IP and URL-shortener warnings, with regression tests and maintainer-approved moderation policy.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. By source inspection, a `.json`, `.yaml`, `.yml`, or `.toml` manifest containing a loopback raw-IP URL such as `http://127.0.0.1:8080/...` flows through `scanManifestFile` and emits `suspicious.install_untrusted_source`; the GHCR image-pull hypothesis itself was not confirmed in source.

## Solution Assessment

Yes, but narrower than the issue’s suggested allowlist or metadata field. A loopback-aware manifest matcher is the smallest maintainable fix unless maintainers explicitly choose a broader verified-registry or docker-compose policy.
