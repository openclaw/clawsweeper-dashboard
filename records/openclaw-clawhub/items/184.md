## Summary

Current main does not provide the requested paid human-review certification. Existing trust mechanisms cover automated scan state, moderator overrides, official skills, and official publishers, while related verification issues remain open or explicitly complementary; this still requires a maintainer product and security decision.

## What This Changes

Add a paid expedited human security-review process that grants approved skills a visible trust badge.

## Best Possible Solution

Define a version-bound, time-limited human-review attestation with reviewer provenance, disclosed scope and limitations, renewal and rescan rules, revocation and appeals, and UI wording that cannot be confused with publisher identity or an unconditional security guarantee.

## Risks / Open Questions

- A paid approval badge could be interpreted as a security warranty unless its review scope, limitations, reviewer authority, and user-facing wording are explicit.
- Review validity needs version binding, expiry, rescan triggers, revocation after new findings, conflict-of-interest controls, and an appeal path.
- Automated scan results, official publisher identity, official skill curation, and human code-review approval must remain visibly distinct trust signals.
