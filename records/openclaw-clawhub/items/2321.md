## Summary

Current main has detailed scanner, visibility, install-blocking, appeal, and account-enforcement mechanics, but it does not resolve this RFC’s central policy questions about prohibited categories, exceptions, or consequence mapping. The collaborator-authored issue is recently active, security-sensitive, and explicitly awaiting product judgment, so it remains necessary.

## What This Changes

Define ClawHub content-policy categories and map them to discovery, installability, human review, and account-enforcement outcomes.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve one policy matrix that separates content eligibility, spam and quality handling, contextual scanner evidence, positive trust signals, artifact visibility and installability, and account escalation; persist internal invariants in `specs/security-moderation.md` and stable public rules in `docs/acceptable-usage.md`.
