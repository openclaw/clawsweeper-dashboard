## Summary

Keep open: current main now documents prohibited abuse categories, enforcement powers, publisher-abuse review, scanner verdicts, and the SkillSpector scraping boundary, but it does not record a completed decision for this RFC’s broader category synthesis—especially low-quality or duplicate content versus malicious abuse, positive trust signals, and whether legitimate crypto or finance uses are categorically excluded. The item is also collaborator-authored and explicitly remains in maintainer review, so it requires a trust-and-safety product decision rather than cleanup closure.

## What This Changes

Define ClawHub content-policy categories and map them to discovery, registry eligibility, upload review, installability, and account enforcement.

## Best Possible Solution

Reach an explicit maintainer decision that separates prohibited abuse, quality or spam review, scanner findings, and positive trust signals, then persist the internal enforcement matrix in `specs/security-moderation.md` and publish only stable user-facing rules in `docs/acceptable-usage.md`.

## Solution Assessment

Yes, keeping a canonical RFC open is the best current path because the implementation and public policy cover adjacent mechanics without resolving all requested category boundaries.
