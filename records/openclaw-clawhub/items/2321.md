## Summary

The repository now has behavior-based acceptable-use rules and mature moderation mechanics, but this collaborator-authored RFC still owns unresolved product choices about category exceptions, discovery/installability effects, trust-signal separation, and account consequences. Recent discussion and the merged scraping-policy adjustment show the policy is actively evolving rather than complete.

## What This Changes

Define content-policy review categories and their effects on public registry inclusion, visibility, installability, human review, and account enforcement.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve a behavior-based category matrix that separately defines content eligibility, audit evidence, quality/trust signals, discovery, installability, and account consequences, then preserve internal rationale in `specs/` and publish stable rules in `docs/`.
