## Summary

Keep this active: it is a collaborator-authored, security-sensitive RFC that still requires explicit maintainer decisions on prohibited content, exceptions, visibility, installability, and account enforcement. Current main documents moderation and audit mechanics but does not settle the proposed category boundaries, and recent discussion provides substantive edge cases rather than evidence that the RFC is complete.

## What This Changes

Define content-policy categories and their effects on registry visibility, upload review, installability, reporting, and account enforcement.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve a category matrix that separately defines content eligibility, audit evidence, quality and trust signals, discovery, installability, and account consequences, with narrow examples and appeal paths; preserve sensitive rationale in `specs/` and publish only stable user-facing rules in `docs/`.

## Solution Assessment
