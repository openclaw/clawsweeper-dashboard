## Summary

This RFC remains necessary. Current main documents prohibited abuse classes and scanner-state semantics, but it does not settle the central product choices around crypto/finance scope, spam exceptions, positive trust signals, discovery/installability effects, or account-level enforcement. The thread also contains substantive false-positive and category-boundary evidence, and its collaborator authorship plus protected security/product-review signals require explicit maintainer handling.

Likely related people:
- Patrick-Erichsen — high confidence; RFC author and primary recent author of the moderation policy/spec surfaces.
- Vincent Koc — medium confidence; recent contributor to moderated skill-version visibility behavior.

## What This Changes

Define content-policy categories for deciding registry visibility, human review, removal, installability, and account enforcement.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this RFC as the canonical decision forum, separate prohibited-content categories from positive trust signals and scanner evidence, then encode approved reviewer invariants in `specs/security-moderation.md` and publish clear user-facing categories and appeal consequences in `docs/acceptable-usage.md` and `docs/moderation.md`.
