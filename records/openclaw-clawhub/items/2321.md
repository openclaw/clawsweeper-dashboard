## Summary

Current main implements detailed scanner, visibility, install-blocking, spam-review, and account-escalation mechanics, but it does not resolve this RFC’s central normative questions about prohibited content categories, exceptions, or the mapping from categories to discovery and account enforcement. The issue is also collaborator-authored, security-sensitive, recently active, and explicitly awaiting product judgment, so it should remain open for maintainer synthesis.

## What This Changes

Define content-policy categories and their effects on registry visibility, upload review, installability, and account enforcement.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this RFC as the canonical decision record, have maintainers synthesize the feedback into distinct enforcement categories and positive trust signals, define concrete allowed, prohibited, and edge-case examples, then persist private enforcement invariants in `specs/security-moderation.md` and stable user-facing rules in `docs/acceptable-usage.md`.
