## Summary

The RFC remains necessary: current main implements scanner verdict and quarantine mechanics, but no settled policy defines the requested content categories or maps them across discovery, installability, review, and account enforcement. Because it is collaborator-authored and carries protected maintainer, product, and security review signals, it requires explicit maintainer disposition.

## What This Changes

Define content-policy categories for spam, finance/on-chain content, and malicious artifacts, including their effects on visibility, review, installability, and account enforcement.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this as the canonical policy thread, approve a category-by-consequence matrix with explicit edge cases and appeals, then encode private invariants in `specs/security-moderation.md` and stable user-facing rules in public moderation or acceptable-use docs.
