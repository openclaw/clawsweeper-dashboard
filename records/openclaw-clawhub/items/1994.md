## Summary

Keep open. Current main distinguishes Official publishers from community packages and exposes provenance and scan signals, but it does not provide a documented public route for a third-party plugin to earn a reviewed or Verified-style status. The broader policy decision remains active in https://github.com/openclaw/clawhub/issues/2272.

## What This Changes

Define how a third-party plugin or publisher can obtain a Verified-style trust status on ClawHub.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep community packages distinct from Official publishers and define a documented, auditable vetted-community status through the canonical RFC, including eligibility, review authority, package-versus-publisher scope, renewal and revocation, security disclosures, and public badge wording.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable; this issue requests a new public verification workflow rather than reporting broken existing behavior. Current source and documentation clearly establish the absence and boundaries of that workflow.

## Solution Assessment

No, granting a one-off Verified status is not the best solution. The narrowest maintainable direction is to settle the general vetted-community policy in https://github.com/openclaw/clawhub/issues/2272 and apply it consistently to eligible plugins.
