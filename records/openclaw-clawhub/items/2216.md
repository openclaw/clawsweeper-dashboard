## Summary

Current main still lacks the requested shared period-popularity contract: skills have a weekly temporal foundation, but publishers and plugins remain on aggregate or catalog sort paths. The RFC is concrete and still necessary, while its scoring, attribution, backfill, and rollout choices require maintainer product judgment.

## What This Changes

Add weekly, monthly, and all-time popularity selection across publisher, skill, and plugin browse surfaces, backed by consistent temporal aggregation and stable pagination.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve and persist one shared period, scoring, attribution, and snapshot contract, then land staged indexed package and publisher daily rollups plus period APIs before adding consistent tabs to all three browse surfaces.
