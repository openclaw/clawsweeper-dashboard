## Summary

Current main still lacks the RFC’s central cross-surface capability: skills have a limited weekly leaderboard foundation, but publishers remain all-time ranked and plugins lack temporal rollups or period browse contracts. The issue is detailed and actionable as an RFC, but scoring, ownership attribution, low-activity behavior, and historical backfill require maintainer product and architecture decisions before implementation.

## What This Changes

Add aligned weekly, monthly, and all-time popularity browsing for publishers, skills, and plugins, backed by temporal statistics and stable pagination.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve one shared period, scoring, attribution, and low-activity contract, then stage indexed package and publisher daily rollups plus snapshot-stable browse APIs before adding synchronized period tabs to the three pages.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable; this is an RFC for new discovery behavior rather than a broken established contract. Source inspection confirms the requested cross-entity period capability is absent on current main.

## Solution Assessment

Yes at the architectural level: bounded daily rollups and snapshot-stable leaderboard pagination are preferable to browse-time event scans. The proposal cannot move directly to implementation until maintainers decide metric, attribution, threshold, identity, and backfill semantics.
