The PR addresses a real current-main false-clean gap, but it is not merge-ready: it conflicts with newer ClawScan visibility work, lacks real-run proof, and makes an unresolved policy choice that every stripped hidden comment must force review.

The branch adds artifact-coverage metadata to prompt-backed ClawScan, floors benign incomplete evaluations to suspicious/review, persists the metadata for skills and packages, and exposes `review.artifact_incomplete` in moderation.

## Best Possible Solution

Rebase the coverage computation onto current main, floor only content classes maintainers explicitly consider materially unreviewed, and demonstrate stored and operator-visible results with redacted real ClawScan runs for truncation and comment-containing artifacts.

Yes. Current-main source shows prompt-backed text is sliced without a coverage marker, and the branch’s focused regressions exercise a benign response after omitted content; no real current-main backend run was performed in this review.

## Solution Assessment

Partly. Computing coverage from trusted prompt assembly and enforcing it after parsing is the narrow maintainable architecture for truncation and omission, but the hidden-comment boundary and semantic rebase still require maintainer judgment.
