## Risks / Open Questions

- A nondeterministic third-party LLM score could become a de facto enforcement signal without defensible calibration, reproducibility, or appeal semantics.
- Submitting unpublished skill content to external model providers could create privacy, licensing, retention, and disclosure concerns.
- A second 0–100 quality score could conflict with ClawHub’s existing structural score unless its name, audience, authority, and relationship to moderation are explicit.
- Behavior tests that execute skill workflows require a clearly defined sandbox and network, credential, filesystem, and cost boundary.
