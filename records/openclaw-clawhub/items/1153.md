## Risks / Open Questions

- Hard deletion is irreversible and can remove version rows, fingerprints, scan jobs, embeddings, comments, reports, stars, badges, stats, installs, and the skill record; the permitted retention and backup boundary needs explicit approval.
- Republishing a different artifact as 1.0.0 changes the effective immutability of an already-published version and could create cache, mirror, audit, or user-expectation ambiguity without a deliberate policy decision.
- Public HTTP removal can verify that records are no longer served, but it cannot by itself prove removal from backups, external mirrors, or retained audit data.
