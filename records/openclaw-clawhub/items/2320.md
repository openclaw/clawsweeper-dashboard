## Summary

The RFC remains necessary: current main has concrete account-purge, publisher migration, and official-publisher mechanics, but its checked-in draft still leaves ownership evidence, dispute adjudication, deleted-namespace disposition, recovery exceptions, and disclosure boundaries unresolved. The live item is collaborator-authored and explicitly awaiting product and security review, while the Mapbox and ClawSec cases remain open.

## What This Changes

Define transparent policy for owner namespace claims and disputes, deleted-owner namespaces, and recovery after owner-initiated account deletion.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve durable rules for claim evidence, dispute handling, inactive or deleted owners, eligible self-delete recovery, enforcement exclusions, auditability, and disclosure; publish the non-sensitive policy in public docs and split admin tooling or data changes into bounded follow-ups.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable: this is a policy RFC rather than a deterministic runtime bug. The open Mapbox and ClawSec reports provide concrete adjudication cases, but they do not resolve the policy choices.

## Solution Assessment

Yes: an RFC followed by durable security specs, public policy docs, and bounded implementation follow-ups is the safest maintainable approach. Existing purge and publisher-admin mechanics are insufficient substitutes for explicit evidence and adjudication rules.
