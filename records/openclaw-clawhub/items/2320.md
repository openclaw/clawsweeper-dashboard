## Summary

The RFC remains necessary because current main implements irreversible deletion, identity cleanup, handle reservations, and official-publisher mechanics, but it still does not define the evidence or adjudication policy for namespace claims and disputes. The issue is collaborator-authored, explicitly under product/security review, and its Mapbox and ClawSec decision cases remain open. Likely related people: Patrick-Erichsen (high confidence), based on current-main authorship of the RFC and adjacent account/reservation work.

## What This Changes

Define transparent policy for organization or owner namespace claims, disputes, inactive-owner handling, and recovery after owner-initiated account deletion.

## Best Possible Solution

Approve durable claim, dispute, inactive-owner, and recovery rules in specs; publish only the non-sensitive policy in public docs; then split any admin tooling, audits, or data cleanup into bounded follow-up work.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable: this is a policy RFC rather than a deterministic runtime bug. The open Mapbox and ClawSec reports provide concrete adjudication cases, but they do not turn the policy choice into a reproducible defect.

## Solution Assessment

Yes: an RFC followed by durable security specs, public policy docs, and bounded implementation follow-ups is the narrowest maintainable approach. Inferring claim or recovery rules from existing database mechanics would leave critical evidence and privacy decisions undocumented.
