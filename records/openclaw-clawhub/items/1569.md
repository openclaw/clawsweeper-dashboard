## Summary

Keep open: ClawHub cleanup policy keeps issues open, and current main still implements the direct `clawhub transfer` CLI/API path as user-to-user. The publish-time `--owner` plus `--migrate-owner` route now covers a republish-based move, but the requested direct personal-to-organization transfer remains tracked by https://github.com/openclaw/clawhub/issues/1675 and the open implementation candidate at https://github.com/openclaw/clawhub/pull/1635.

## What This Changes

The issue requests a way to transfer skills from a personal publisher to an organization so other organization users can publish and update them.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the explicit publish-time migration route as the supported immediate path, then land or extract a publisher-targeted direct transfer flow with role checks, audit logging, alias preservation, and regression coverage if the transfer command/API remains a product requirement.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source inspection gives a high-confidence path: the direct transfer command/API sends `toUserHandle`, the backend resolves only users or personal publishers, and an organization handle falls through to `User not found`.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear as a final product direction. The merged `--owner` plus `--migrate-owner` publish path is maintainable for owners who can republish, but direct org transfers need a publisher-targeted transfer model rather than a small resolver change.
