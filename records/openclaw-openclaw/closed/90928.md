## Summary

Keep open: current main and the latest release still initialize the Android onboarding QR scanner through the Google Play services code-scanner path, so the no-GMS/HarmonyOS launch fallback is not implemented yet.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to support HarmonyOS/Huawei Android devices without GMS by letting the Android companion app launch and pair without Google Mobile Services.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the Android app usable on no-GMS devices by making QR scanning optional or lazily unavailable while preserving setup-code/manual pairing; track AppGallery or Huawei Push work separately if maintainers want it.

## Reproduction Assessment

No live high-confidence reproduction was established in this read-only review. Source inspection shows first-run onboarding still creates a Google Play services scanner client, and a redacted HarmonyOS logcat trace would make the crash path high confidence.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear for the full support request, but the best first repair is narrow: degrade QR scanning gracefully without GMS and keep setup-code/manual pairing available. AppGallery publishing or Huawei Push Kit support should be a separate maintainer-scoped product decision.
