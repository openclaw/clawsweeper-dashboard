## Summary

Current `main` already covers the reported macOS LaunchAgent stop/start failure: normal stop no longer bootouts the service, and start/restart now recover an installed-but-unloaded LaunchAgent by re-enabling and re-bootstrapping it from the plist.

## Evidence

- **Normal macOS stop no longer removes the LaunchAgent in the healthy path:** `stopLaunchAgent` now uses `launchctl disable` plus `launchctl stop`, and only falls back to `bootout` in degraded/error paths. That directly avoids the original "stop removes service registration" behavior for normal stop/start cycles.
