## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` does not implement the requested stale-session cleanup: the restore path still just logs and skips old calls without hanging up the provider leg or persisting a terminal/purged record, and restore-time max-duration timers still restart for a full fresh window rather than the remaining duration.

## Evidence

- **Stale restored calls are only skipped, not purged or hung up:** `CallManager.verifyRestoredCalls()` logs `Skipping restored call ... (older than maxDurationSeconds)` and immediately `continue`s. There is no `provider.hangupCall(...)`, `finalizeCall(...)`, or persistence update in that branch.
  - file: [extensions/voice-call/src/manager.ts:158](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/4013c658537ee2051c44186f197b0710e84c9049/extensions/voice-call/src/manager.ts#L158)
  - command: `nl -ba extensions/voice-call/src/manager.ts | sed -n '138,164p'`
  - sha: [4013c658537e](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/4013c658537ee2051c44186f197b0710e84c9049)
- **Persisted non-terminal calls remain eligible on every restart:** `loadActiveCallsFromStore()` rebuilds active calls from the latest non-terminal record in `calls.jsonl`. Because the stale-skip branch writes nothing back, an old non-terminal record remains in the store and will be reconsidered after later restarts.
  - file: [extensions/voice-call/src/manager/store.ts:27](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/4013c658537ee2051c44186f197b0710e84c9049/extensions/voice-call/src/manager/store.ts#L27)

Otherwise `startMaxDurationTimer()` is called with no remaining-time parameter, and `startMaxDurationTimer()` always schedules `setTimeout(..., maxDurationSeconds * 1000)`.
