## Label Justifications

## Summary

Keep open: current main and the latest shipped release still do not prove Slack replies with concrete platform receipts before treating delivery as successful, and the linked fixing PR is still open and conflicting rather than merged. Recent production comments, including a v2026.6.6 report, show the transcript-success/platform-delivery mismatch still matters.

## What This Changes

The issue reports Slack replies being finalized in OpenClaw transcripts but not posted to Slack, asking for visible delivery failures, safer in-flight handling, and Slack receipt confirmation.

## Best Possible Solution

Refresh and review https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/pull/80749 or a narrow successor so Slack final replies are not marked delivered without a concrete platform receipt or durable failure record, while keeping broader cross-channel outbox semantics in https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/87561.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes at source/live-report confidence, but not as a local live Slack repro in this read-only review. Current source still accepts ambiguous Slack identities and marks delivery observed after `deliverReplies`, and the issue has fresh v2026.6.6 production evidence of the same mismatch.

## Solution Assessment

Yes, the best narrow solution is receipt/failure accounting in the Slack delivery path before marking replies delivered. Unconditional auto-promotion of private final text would conflict with the current `message_tool_only` policy and should stay out of the narrow fix unless maintainers choose a separate product change.
