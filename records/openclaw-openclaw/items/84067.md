## Summary

## What This Changes

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the structured-code propagation, remove or narrow the broad natural-language tokens, and either preserve the documented exact-code fallback or explicitly update docs/tests after maintainer policy sign-off, with real cron proof before merge.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Current main source and tests show denial-looking cron prose is promoted to a fatal cron error, and the related open issue provides a concrete false-positive example for `could not run` prose.

## Solution Assessment

No, not as-is. Structured denial signals are the right direction, but dropping every documented marker fallback is broader than the demonstrated false-positive fix unless maintainers explicitly choose that policy and update docs/tests.
