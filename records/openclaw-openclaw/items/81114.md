Fix https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/81114 on current main. Keep the change narrow to the Codex app-server runner: after `turn/start`, do not let the original `params.timeoutMs` wall clock abort and retire a turn that is still producing current-turn activity. Preserve hard-dead/stuck-turn protection through the terminal watchdog, preserve account/rate-limit non-turn notifications as non-progress, and keep `appServer.requestTimeoutMs` as a control-plane/startup/request budget rather than a progress-blind accepted-turn cap. Add regression coverage with a fake app-server client where current-turn notifications continue past the old attempt timeout and the turn later completes successfully, plus coverage that a silent/dead turn still times out and is interrupted. Avoid broad provider fallback/model-selection changes and avoid adding a new config option unless the existing documented Codex app-server config cannot express the needed behavior.
