## Summary

Current main now provides provider quota-window visibility and reactive quota suspension, but it does not implement predictive pace tiers or proactive throttling at safe autonomous-work boundaries. Those remaining behaviors are meaningful product work and need maintainer policy decisions rather than stale cleanup.

## What This Changes

Add projected provider-quota pacing and proactive autonomous-agent throttling on top of existing usage-window visibility.

## Best Possible Solution

Extend the canonical provider-usage model with advisory projections first, then enforce policy only at explicit spawn or scheduling boundaries after maintainers define provider coverage, thresholds, defaults, persistence ownership, and recovery behavior.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable: this issue requests new predictive and enforcement behavior rather than reporting a broken existing contract. Current main's adjacent visibility and reactive suspension behavior are directly verifiable from source.

## Solution Assessment

Partly: reusing the existing usage-window and suspension primitives is the narrowest maintainable foundation, but the proposed fixed tier formula and automatic blocking policy are not yet proven to be the safest design.
