## Summary

Keep open: current main has improved durable final delivery and polling liveness, but it still leaves Telegram recovery gaps for polling-restart queue drains and streamed follow-up chunks; the latest WSL2 report and related open items show the symptom family is still active.

## What This Changes

Request that Telegram outbound replies survive polling stalls by retrying safe send failures, preserving ambiguous failures, and draining eligible queued delivery without requiring a gateway restart.

## Best Possible Solution

Land a maintainer-approved Telegram delivery recovery design that durably records final and follow-up chunks, drains eligible queued work after polling recovery, and preserves ambiguous sends instead of blindly replaying them.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level. Simulate a long streamed Telegram final where remaining follow-up chunks fail during polling recovery, or a queued final send left pending after a stall; current main lacks a Telegram drain hook and leaves follow-up chunks on a direct send path.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. The durable queue is the right foundation, but a broad retry or one-line classifier change is not the best full fix without a duplicate-safe classification and polling-restart drain contract.
