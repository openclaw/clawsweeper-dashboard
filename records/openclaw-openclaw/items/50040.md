## Summary

Keep open: current main has important partial fixes for durable final sends and safe Telegram retry classification, but the broader polling-recovery/outbound-loss gap is still present. Telegram polling restarts do not trigger a Telegram delivery-queue drain, streamed final follow-up chunks still use a direct non-durable send path, and Telegram does not declare unknown-send reconciliation needed for safe replay after an ambiguous send attempt.

## What This Changes

The issue asks for Telegram outbound delivery recovery during polling stalls, including runtime retry/drain behavior, failure classification, and stateful pending/ambiguous delivery handling.

## Best Possible Solution

Add a maintainer-approved Telegram durable-final recovery design that makes all final chunks queue-aware, triggers eligible queue drains after polling recovery, and preserves or reconciles unknown-after-send entries without blind duplicate sends.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level. A Telegram streamed final follow-up chunk still sends without durable:true, and a queued Telegram delivery has no polling-restart drain path; ambiguous post-send entries also lack Telegram reconciliation for safe replay.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. The shared durable queue is the right substrate, but the remaining fix needs maintainer decisions for duplicate-safe replay, unknown-send reconciliation, and exactly when Telegram polling recovery should drain queued work.
