    Every-schedule jobs did not enforce the minimum refire gap after
    execution.  When job execution time exceeded the interval (e.g. a 5s
    interval job taking 17s), the anchor-based next-run computation would
    land at or before endedAt, making the job immediately due again and
    creating a tight refire loop.
    
    For every-schedule jobs, only apply the 2s refire floor when the
    computed next run lands at or before endedAt (execution exceeded the
    interval). When naturalNext is already past endedAt, the job keeps
    its natural cadence. Cron-expression schedules keep the unconditional
    Math.max guard for timezone/croner edge cases (#17821). Malformed
    every-schedules (undefined naturalNext) stay unscheduled rather than
    falling back to minNext.
    
    Closes #52097
