## Summary

Keep open: current main and v2026.6.5 subscribe to Telegram Business update types through grammY defaults, but neither processes Business messages/connections nor provides complete Business reply routing; the prior implementation was closed unmerged and its fork mirror is not a safe canonical replacement.

## What This Changes

Add Telegram Business-connected chat support for inbound Business updates and outbound replies made on behalf of the connected account.

## Best Possible Solution

Add plugin-owned, account-scoped Telegram Business lifecycle support using canonical SQLite state, route supported Business messages through the existing inbound authorization pipeline, carry prepared connection context into supported outbound calls, clear disabled connections, and prove fresh-connect, restart, disconnect, multi-account, and real Telegram reply behavior.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, from source inspection with high confidence: Business update types are subscribed through grammY defaults, but current main and v2026.6.5 register no Business message/connection handlers. The discussion also contains a sustained real-world patched-runtime report, although this review did not connect a live Business account.

## Solution Assessment

No, the initially suggested allowed-update additions and process-local map are not the best complete fix. The maintainable solution needs plugin-owned durable state, account isolation, disconnect cleanup, existing DM authorization integration, and method-specific outbound context.

## Risks / Open Questions

- The durable key must isolate Telegram account, Business connection, and peer chat so multi-account installations cannot apply another account's connection ID.
- Disconnect or permission-change updates must invalidate stored routing immediately; otherwise stale credentials may be attached to later outbound calls.
- Treating every Business message as an ordinary trusted DM could expose personal-chat content or bypass the existing Telegram authorization model without an explicit product decision.
- A generic API transformer may add `business_connection_id` to methods that do not accept it or to unrelated sends unless supported methods and prepared routing context are modeled explicitly.
