## Summary

Keep open: current main still preserves fresh claims owned by another live process, and the direct fixes for this live-owned Telegram ingress deadlock are open rather than merged. The issue has strong source-level support and should stay open until one canonical fix lands or maintainers intentionally choose another recovery policy.

## What This Changes

The issue reports Telegram isolated ingress claims held by a live or non-progressing worker, leaving `channel_ingress_events` rows claimed and same-lane inbound messages pending until gateway restart or manual SQLite cleanup.

## Best Possible Solution

Review and consolidate one linked Telegram live-owned claim recovery fix that preserves active-handler protection, uses token-guarded durable queue mutation, adds focused regression coverage, and closes the issue only after merge.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level. Current main still has a regression test that preserves a fresh other-live-owner Telegram claim and leaves the later same-lane update blocked, matching the reported claimed/pending SQLite state.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear for the final policy, but the linked PR direction is the right owner boundary. Maintainers still need to choose whether timed-out live-owned claims should be failed, released for retry, or handled through a broader stale-ingress recovery rule.
