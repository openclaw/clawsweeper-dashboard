## Label Justifications

## Summary

Keep this PR open, but do not merge it as written: it fixes the swallowed-failure signal, yet its immediate three-attempt limit can turn a brief provider or network outage into permanent Telegram message loss, and the supplied proof does not exercise a real Telegram transport.

## What This Changes

The PR records swallowed Telegram message-processing failures, marks their update tracker entries incomplete, retries isolated-ingress spool entries, and dead-letters them after three failed attempts.

## Best Possible Solution

Carry a per-update failure outcome to the spool owner, but preserve retryable updates with a meaningful delayed/backoff policy and reserve dead-lettering for classified permanent failures or exhaustion over a deliberate time window, with live Telegram proof of failure, retry, and final delivery behavior.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Current main clearly deletes the durable spool record after `handleUpdate()` resolves while the message-processing and grammY error paths can swallow the underlying failure, and the PR body demonstrates that path with real SQLite spool output.

## Solution Assessment

No, not as written. Propagating an explicit failure outcome is appropriate, but unconditional dead-lettering after three immediate attempts is less durable than a classified or delayed retry policy and can recreate message loss during brief outages.
