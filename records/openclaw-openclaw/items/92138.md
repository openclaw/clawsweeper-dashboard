## Summary

Current source supports a plausible cross-contact failure: inbound LIDs and outbound phone targets are resolved through separate reverse and forward mapping paths without an evident consistency check, and pending delivery remains durable when delivery does not complete. No later fix or safe canonical replacement was found, while the reported production disclosure and session corruption require maintainer-led security review and a live, redacted WhatsApp reproduction before implementation.

## What This Changes

Investigate and prevent WhatsApp LID/phone identity cross-routing that can deliver replies to the wrong contact and corrupt another session’s pending delivery state.

## Best Possible Solution

Make one account-scoped identity resolver own both LID→phone and phone→LID routing, require forward/reverse agreement before selecting a contact, fail closed with an explicit retryable delivery error on ambiguity, and verify that ingress, outbound delivery, and pending-final recovery preserve the same intended recipient.

## Reproduction Assessment

Partially yes: source inspection provides a concrete path where inconsistent forward and reverse LID mappings can select different contact identities, and the report supplies detailed production artifacts. A high-confidence full reproduction still requires a controlled current-main WhatsApp account demonstrating the wrong raw JID, session key, and delivery target without exposing private data.

## Solution Assessment

No, the issue’s tentative mapping-file or queue-specific fixes are not yet narrow enough to call the best solution. The maintainable fix is to enforce one verified identity invariant at the WhatsApp owner boundary and then prove inbound routing, outbound targeting, and failed-delivery recovery together.
