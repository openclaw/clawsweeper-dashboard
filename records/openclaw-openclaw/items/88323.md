## Best Possible Solution

Keep generated-artifact idempotency, but scope it to the duplicate replay group or delivery-affecting route semantics and record the artifact only after confirmed send success.

## Solution Assessment

## Review Findings

Overall correctness: patch is incorrect

Overall confidence: 0.86

Full review comments:

- **[P1] Keep target scope in generated-media dedupe keys:** `src/infra/outbound/generated-media-dedupe.ts:57-63`
  - body: `resolveGeneratedMediaDeliveryKeys` receives the route but then serializes every route field as `null`. Because the guard is called from the general send action and durable send paths, once a generated artifact is sent to one target, a later legitimate send of that same artifact to another channel/thread/account within the TTL is treated as a duplicate and suppressed. Include delivery-affecting scope in the key or restrict route-agnostic suppression to the specific same-turn replay path.
  - confidence: 0.88
- **[P1] Record dispatcher finals only after delivery succeeds:** `src/auto-reply/reply/dispatch-from-config.ts:2014-2017`
  - body: This marks the generated artifact as delivered immediately after `sendFinalReply` queues the payload, but `sendFinalReply` only enqueues work; the dispatcher records failures later through `waitForIdle()`/`getFailedCounts()`. A replay path that arrives while the queued send later fails will be suppressed, losing the generated media. Move the mark after the existing idle/failure check confirms the final delivery was not cancelled or failed.
  - confidence: 0.84
