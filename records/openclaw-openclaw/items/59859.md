## Review Findings

Overall correctness: patch is incorrect

Overall confidence: 0.9

Full review comments:

- **[P1] Preserve non-cron schedules when editing jobs:** `apps/linux/src/section_cron.c:274-275`
  - body: The edit save path always writes `schedule.kind = "cron"` and puts the entry text in `expr`, but current cron jobs may be `at` or `every`. Saving one of those jobs after changing unrelated fields rewrites the schedule shape and can fail validation or change when it runs.
  - confidence: 0.93
- **[P2] Keep session-scoped cron targets on save:** `apps/linux/src/section_cron.c:264-266`
  - body: The selected combo index is serialized through `session_target_from_index`, while `session:<id>` targets are treated as unknown and selected as index 0. Editing a session-scoped job therefore saves it back as `isolated`, retargeting the job away from its intended session.
  - confidence: 0.9
- **[P2] Send supported verbose levels from the session selector:** `apps/linux/src/section_sessions.c:176-184`
  - body: The selector sends `none`, `low`, and `high`, but `sessions.patch` normalizes `verboseLevel` to `off`, `on`, or `full`. Changing verbose from this UI path will be rejected by the gateway.
  - confidence: 0.91
- **[P2] Bracket IPv6 hosts before composing URLs:** `apps/linux/src/gateway_config.c:1153-1155`
  - body: Dashboard URLs interpolate `config->host` directly into `scheme://host:port`; IPv6 literals like `::1` produce malformed URLs such as `http://::1:18789/`, breaking dashboard and log handoffs on valid IPv6 setups.
  - confidence: 0.85
