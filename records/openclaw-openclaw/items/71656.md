## Summary

Keep open. Current main has meaningful partial fixes for transcript noise and scan cursoring, but the central flat ingestion scores, content-level dedupe/importance scoring, and session-corpus promotion boundary are still unresolved and not fully superseded by related items.

## What This Changes

The issue asks memory-core Dreaming to pre-filter noisy session corpus input, score entries by content importance and recency, and keep session corpus scanning fair as the corpus grows.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this as the canonical memory-core Dreaming corpus-quality tracker, then coordinate a memory-core repair that preserves useful transcript ingestion while adding content-level dedupe, bounded content/recency scoring, and fair persisted scan progress.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, source-level: current main still has fixed session/daily ingestion scores, timestamp-or-line-scoped dedupe, and capped session scanning visible in the Dreaming source. The exact April 9 freeze is not reproducible without the reporter's private memory/.dreams state.

## Solution Assessment
