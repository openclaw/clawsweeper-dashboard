## Summary

Keep open. Current main has partial transcript denoising and session cursoring, but the central fixed ingestion scores, generic daily session signals, and unresolved session-corpus promotion boundary are still present; related open issues cover adjacent symptoms but do not supersede this broader tracker.

## What This Changes

The issue asks for memory-core Dreaming to filter noisy session corpus material, score entries by content quality and recency, and rotate scan budget fairly across corpus sources.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this as the broad memory-core Dreaming corpus-quality tracker, then coordinate a repair that preserves useful transcript ingestion while adding content-level dedupe/filtering, bounded source-aware scoring with recency, and fair persisted scan progress under a clear session-corpus promotion contract.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level: current main still uses fixed 0.58/0.62 ingestion scores and generic daily recall signals for session-corpus and daily entries. The exact April 9 stall is not high-confidence reproducible without the reporter's private memory/.dreams state.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. A narrow internal repair is the right direction for filtering, scoring, and scan fairness, but maintainers should first settle whether session-corpus remains direct promotion evidence or becomes internal-only source material.
