## Summary

Keep open. Current main still contains the internal WebChat/Codex routing guard that can keep an external-origin final reply on the internal dispatcher unless an explicit delivery route is carried, and the issue has no merged canonical fix yet.

## What This Changes

A Signal-originated direct turn should deliver the normal final assistant reply back to the original Signal route even when execution resumes on a Codex, WebChat, VS Code, or other internal surface.

## Best Possible Solution

Carry the original inbound delivery route through internal/Codex resumed turns as an explicit source-delivery route, while preserving the existing WebChat/admin guard for turns that are truly internal-only.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes at source level, but not as a live Signal run. Signal inbound contexts preserve the route, and current routing code plus tests show that an internal WebChat/Codex surface will not route external-origin final replies unless ExplicitDeliverRoute is true.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear as an implementation plan. The right direction is to preserve the inbound Signal delivery invariant, but the safest fix is likely to carry or derive ExplicitDeliverRoute for user-originated internal continuations rather than deleting the internal WebChat guard.
