## Summary

Keep open: current main still has only per-surface SSRF policy plumbing, while the requested gateway-wide policy and MS Teams/Auth-refresh coverage remain missing and security-sensitive.

## What This Changes

Request a gateway-level SSRF policy/default, with per-subsystem overrides and at least MS Teams parity for fake-IP/private-network opt-in.

## Best Possible Solution

Design an additive, default-strict SSRF policy hierarchy that is owned by the right runtime/plugin seams, preserves existing per-surface overrides, and includes focused upgrade/security tests plus live fake-IP proof before merge.

## Reproduction Assessment

No live reproduction was performed, but source inspection gives a high-confidence source-level reproduction of the missing capability: current main has no gateway.ssrfPolicy, MS Teams has no network policy config, and OpenAI OAuth refresh calls fetchWithSsrFGuard without a policy.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear until maintainers choose the policy hierarchy. A global default may be the right product shape, but the safest implementation should first define default-strict merge semantics and plugin/provider seams rather than only plumbing a top-level boolean.
