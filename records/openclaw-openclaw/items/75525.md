Yes. On current main, a tool-result message with small visible `content` and large `details` is charged through `estimateUnknownChars(details)`, which can trigger cloning/truncation pressure even though details are stripped before provider submission.

## Solution Assessment

Yes, with the changelog gap fixed. Ignoring details in this estimator is the narrow maintainable fix because the LLM-boundary and compaction token paths already strip tool-result details; persistence and truncation policy do not need to change.
