## Summary

Keep this open: the report matches a plausible current-main delivery gap where an internal continuation can produce a final transcript answer without an external WhatsApp send, and the related open items do not clearly supersede this narrower final-delivery failure.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to ensure WhatsApp-originated internal continuations deliver their final answer back to the original chat or fail/warn visibly instead of silently persisting transcript-only output.

## Best Possible Solution

Define one continuation delivery contract for channel-bound sessions: preserve or derive the source delivery context, require message-tool evidence when policy says message-tool-only, and fail or warn visibly when a final answer cannot be delivered.

## Reproduction Assessment

No; there is not a high-confidence live reproduction from this review. Source inspection does show a plausible current-main path where a runtime-only continuation in a WhatsApp group can finish session-only unless the resumed turn sends through the message tool.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear; the desired behavior is correct, but the maintainable fix needs a policy decision across internal continuation, active subagent handoff, and group message-tool-only delivery. The safer direction is a shared delivery-evidence guard rather than a WhatsApp-only special case.
