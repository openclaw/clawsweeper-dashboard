## Summary

Current main and v2026.6.5 solve substantial parts of the proposal through active-run presence and durable queued final delivery, but they do not establish the requested cross-channel operator alert when delivery finally fails or when an operator-triggered turn produces no outbound response. This canonical product thread should remain open for maintainers to decide whether those remaining guarantees belong in core and what user-visible defaults are acceptable.

## What This Changes

Add runtime-enforced long-turn visibility and end-to-end response delivery confirmation with retry, duplicate protection, persistence, and operator alerts.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep existing presence and SQLite-backed durable delivery as the canonical mechanisms, then define a narrow capability-aware failure-notification and zero-visible-response policy with an explicit alert destination and no blind replay.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable as one reproducible current-main bug. The original silence symptoms have shipped fixes, while the remaining request is a new cross-channel guarantee for final failure and zero-response alerts.

## Solution Assessment

No, the proposed single default-enabled gateway subsystem is not the best implementation shape. Current presence and durable delivery paths should remain canonical, with only the missing alert policy added after maintainers define destinations, channel capabilities, and final-only compatibility.

## Risks / Open Questions

- A default text watchdog could violate channels or configurations that intentionally provide final-only delivery and would add another large config/default surface.
- Operator alerting after primary delivery failure needs a separately reliable destination; alerting through the same failed transport can provide a false guarantee.
- Generic replay is unsafe for channels that cannot reconcile an unknown platform send, because retrying can duplicate a response already accepted by the provider.
