## Summary

Keep open: current main still has the reported sticky auto-fallback behavior, and it is documented and tested as intentional cache-friendly policy rather than an obsolete bug. The remaining question is what recovery policy maintainers want after a primary-model cooldown expires.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to recover from an auto-selected fallback back to the configured primary after the primary model cooldown expires.

## Best Possible Solution

Define the intended recovery policy for `modelOverrideSource: "auto"` after cooldown expiry, then align candidate ordering, session inheritance/reset behavior, diagnostics, docs, and focused tests.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. A source-level reproduction is clear: create a session or parent session with an auto fallback override and an expired primary auth cooldown; current selection preserves or inherits the fallback, and `runWithModelFallback` starts from that fallback before considering the configured primary.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. Retrying the primary first after cooldown would address the report, but it conflicts with current documented behavior; a cooldown-aware or configurable auto-fallback recovery policy may be the safer maintainable path.
