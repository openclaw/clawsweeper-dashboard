## Risks / Open Questions

- Static headers are forgeable and cannot satisfy the stated verification goal; signed claims require an explicit root of trust, protected key ownership, public-key distribution, audience rules, expiry, replay resistance, rotation, and revocation semantics.
- The requested outbound scope is ambiguous across guarded web fetch, provider transports, plugin tools, browser automation, and subprocess-based skills; partial coverage could create inconsistent or misleading guarantees.
- Stable agent or sender identifiers could leak across origins, redirects, proxies, logs, and third-party services unless disclosure and redirect non-forwarding rules are explicit.
- A host-signed token alone cannot prove autonomous-agent rather than human control unless the threat model defines a trusted execution boundary that OpenClaw actually provides.
