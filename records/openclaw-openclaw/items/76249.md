## Summary

Keep open. Current main enforces the byte-flush and active-transcript guards on the primary session JSONL, while the reporter clarified that the wedge correlates with the separate trajectory sidecar; I did not find proof that current main fixes the post-`stream-ready` no-output failure or that this is safely covered by the merged WhatsApp stale-socket fix.

## What This Changes

The issue reports long-lived WhatsApp turns silently stopping after `stream-ready` when `*.trajectory.jsonl` grows past a few MB despite transcript byte-flush settings.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this open, collect the sanitized trajectory sidecars and journal excerpt, then fix the trajectory/embedded-run path so long-lived sessions either avoid the stall or surface and recover from it with clear guidance for trajectory retention.

## Reproduction Assessment

No high-confidence current-main reproduction. The reporter gives concrete v2026.4.29 WhatsApp steps and two primary-vs-sidecar size pairs, but this review only verified static source paths and did not reproduce the post-`stream-ready` turn loss.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. The existing primary transcript byte guards are the right mechanism for active JSONL growth, but they do not address trajectory sidecar growth; the maintainable fix likely belongs in trajectory serialization/retention or embedded-run stuck-turn recovery after logs identify the failing step.
