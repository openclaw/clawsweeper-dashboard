## Summary

Keep open: current main and v2026.6.6 still route first-run Android onboarding through Google Play services code-scanner setup, while the existing third-party flavor is permission-focused rather than a no-GMS/HarmonyOS build. The actionable core is a narrow no-GMS scanner fallback that preserves setup-code/manual pairing; Huawei Push Kit and AppGallery publishing should stay separate unless maintainers explicitly scope them.

## What This Changes

The issue requests HarmonyOS/Huawei Android support without Google Mobile Services, including graceful no-GMS behavior, a GMS-free build, or Huawei distribution/push alternatives.

## Best Possible Solution

Make QR scanning optional or lazily unavailable on no-GMS devices while preserving setup-code/manual pairing in the existing Android app, and track AppGallery or Huawei Push support separately if maintainers want that distribution work.

## Reproduction Assessment

No high-confidence live device reproduction is available; the source-supported path is clear because first-run onboarding creates a Google Play services scanner and official docs say that scanner is powered by Google Play services. A redacted HarmonyOS logcat trace would raise this to high confidence.

## Solution Assessment

Yes for the narrow fix: graceful no-GMS degradation for QR scanning is the smallest maintainable repair because setup-code/manual pairing already exists. A GMS-free release flavor, AppGallery publishing, or Huawei Push Kit support is separate product scope.
