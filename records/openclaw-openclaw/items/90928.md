## Summary

This should stay open: current main still has a first-run Android onboarding path that eagerly creates a Google Play services barcode scanner, and the existing third-party flavor is permission-focused rather than a no-GMS/HarmonyOS distribution. The narrow actionable part is keeping setup-code/manual pairing usable without GMS; AppGallery or Huawei Push support should remain out of that repair unless maintainers explicitly scope it.

## What This Changes

The issue reports that the Android companion app crashes on HarmonyOS/Huawei devices without Google Mobile Services and asks for a GMS-free or gracefully degraded path.

## Best Possible Solution

Make QR scanning optional or lazily unavailable on no-GMS devices while preserving setup-code/manual pairing, then track any AppGallery or Huawei Push work separately if maintainers want it.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes at source level, but not as a live device repro: first-run onboarding eagerly initializes a Google Play services scanner and the report says HarmonyOS lacks GMS classes. A redacted logcat trace from a Huawei/HarmonyOS device would make this high confidence.

## Solution Assessment

The narrowest maintainable fix is graceful no-GMS degradation for QR scanning while keeping manual/setup-code pairing available. A GMS-free release flavor, AppGallery publishing, or Huawei Push support is separate product scope.
