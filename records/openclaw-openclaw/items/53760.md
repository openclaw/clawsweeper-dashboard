## Summary

Keep open. Current main still enforces this policy through group-scoped tool-policy resolution only; direct sessions do not produce group context, Telegram direct config accepts `toolsBySender` without a direct enforcement path, and Signal exposes `toolsBySender` only under group config.

## What This Changes

The issue requests enforceable `toolsBySender` policies for allowlisted DM senders on channels such as Signal and Telegram.

## Best Possible Solution

Add a shared direct-message sender tool-policy resolver beside the group resolver, backed by verified inbound sender identity, documented config/schema semantics, and regressions for Telegram and Signal allowlisted DM senders.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source inspection on current main gives a high-confidence path: a direct Telegram or Signal session has no group context, `resolveGroupToolPolicy` returns no policy, and both core and bundled tool filtering consume only that group-scoped result.

## Solution Assessment

Yes on direction, unclear on exact contract. Extending enforceable sender-aware policy to DMs is the right product boundary, but maintainers need to settle identity trust, wildcard precedence, and coordination with https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/48304 and https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/13948 before implementation.
