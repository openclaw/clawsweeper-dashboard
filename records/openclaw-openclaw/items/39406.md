## Summary

Keep open: current main and v2026.6.6 provide the broad `messages.suppressToolErrors` opt-out, but the requested normal-chat `all | final | off` policy remains a product/config decision for recovered mutating tool failures after successful-looking replies.

## What This Changes

The issue requests a config-backed delivery policy to suppress, delay, or disable transient tool-error warning bubbles after the agent recovers and sends a useful final reply.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this as the canonical maintainer decision point, then implement the chosen warning-delivery policy with config/schema/docs alignment and focused payload plus channel regression coverage.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level. Current policy and tests show mutating tool warnings still appear after success-looking replies unless the broad suppression flag is enabled or the assistant explicitly acknowledges the failed action.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear until maintainers choose the product policy. The existing broad `messages.suppressToolErrors` flag is a useful workaround, but the requested `final` mode changes the safety boundary for failed mutating actions after optimistic assistant text.
