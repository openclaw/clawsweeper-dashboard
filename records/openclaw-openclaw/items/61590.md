## Summary

Keep open. Current main has the expected Telegram voice-note capability and auto-TTS plumbing, but the remaining report is about a normal-turn policy/routing mismatch, and the discussion does not yet identify a narrow current-main failure that can be closed or repaired automatically. The April 25 maintainer comment already says the main primitives are covered while this issue should stay open until there is a concrete repro or a policy/docs split.

## What This Changes

Investigate why Telegram TTS voice can succeed in explicit tests but fail to arrive consistently during normal assistant replies when `messages.tts.auto` and reply-routing policy are involved.

## Best Possible Solution

Define the official Telegram normal-reply TTS contract in terms of `always`, `inbound`, `tagged`, session overrides, and streaming, then fix the smallest identified mismatch in speech-core auto gating or Telegram delivery with focused regression coverage.

## Reproduction Assessment

No. We have source and test evidence for explicit voice delivery and auto-TTS gating, but no current-main config/log/transcript that reproduces a normal Telegram reply where voice was expected and skipped.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. Keeping this open for a focused repro or contract clarification is the best current path; changing the broad auto-TTS gates without proof would risk breaking intended `always`/`inbound`/`tagged` semantics.
