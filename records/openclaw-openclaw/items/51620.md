- `impact:message-loss`: The reported failure is queued, in-flight, and drain-window inbound channel messages being lost across restart.
- `impact:session-state`: The root behavior involves process-local followup queue and active session state disappearing or being rejected during gateway lifecycle transitions.

## Summary

Keep open: current main and the latest release still use process-local followup queues, reject drain-window enqueues without durable replay, and the open recovery PRs have not merged. The issue discussion and related PRs point to a real reliability gap, but the final replay contract still needs maintainer review.

## What This Changes

The issue requests durable preservation and replay of queued, in-flight, and drain-window inbound messages across gateway restarts.

## Best Possible Solution

Land one maintainer-approved durable restart recovery contract that persists accepted followups and drain-window arrivals, replays them through typed untrusted dispatch paths with idempotent dedupe, and leaves provider history catch-up or ACK policy to narrower provider-owned work.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, source-level: a pending item in FOLLOWUP_QUEUES is process-local and disappears across a real process restart, while markGatewayDraining makes new command enqueues reject with GatewayDrainingError and current main has no durable pending-message replay path.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear for the full request: persisting accepted followups is the narrow first repair, but drain-window arrivals, provider ACK/catch-up, replay ordering, dedupe, and local-state security need a typed maintainer-approved contract rather than ad hoc replay.
