## Summary

Keep open: current main and v2026.6.10 still expose `sessions_yield` as a subagent-oriented turn-ending primitive, while isolated cron completion remains pollable/delivery-state based rather than a general parent-session wake. The open docs candidate at https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/pull/95569 and the runtime-contract RFC at https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/96190 need maintainer handling before this issue can close.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to clarify or change `sessions_yield` behavior when a yielded session is waiting for isolated cron-run completion.

## Best Possible Solution

Land or reject the docs-only clarification deliberately, and keep the opt-in runtime wake/API decision in https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/96190 unless maintainers choose to close the behavior as documented-only.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source inspection shows `sessions_yield` records yield intent and needs a later event, while isolated cron completion does not generally enqueue a parent-session wake; the reporter also provided a dated live reproduction from June 21, 2026.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear until maintainers choose the contract. A docs/tool-text clarification is the lowest-risk mitigation, while a wait selector or cron notify option would add new session/cron API surface that needs explicit product approval.
