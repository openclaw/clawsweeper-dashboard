## Summary

Current main solves the central user problem: raw or raw-derived provider failures are sanitized at user-facing delivery boundaries, generic non-direct failures can be silent, and invalid persisted thinking signatures have repair and retry handling. Provider-specific residual cases are tracked by narrower open reports, while automatic cross-agent escalation is a separate product proposal rather than a reason to keep this umbrella open.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to prevent raw LLM/API errors from reaching users and proposes retry, silence, cross-agent escalation, and incompatible-session recovery.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the centralized sanitized external-error boundary and thinking-signature recovery, and handle any reproducible provider-specific remainder in the narrower canonical issues rather than reopening a broad retry/escalation umbrella.

## Solution Assessment

Yes for the central issue: sanitizing only at the user-facing boundary preserves internal diagnostics, and known invalid thinking signatures are handled in the transcript recovery path; automatic cross-agent escalation should remain a separate product decision.
