## Label Justifications

- `P2`: The missing deterministic control affects real multi-agent channel workflows but is a bounded product enhancement rather than an urgent runtime outage.
- `impact:message-loss`: The issue concerns whether sub-agent completion messages are delivered directly, intentionally suppressed, or routed away from the requester channel.

## Summary

Current main still has no public framework-level way to deterministically skip a normal sub-agent completion announcement: suppression remains output-token driven, while the only structural bypass is internal completion ownership for specific ACP flows. The need is valid, but the overlapping skip, parent-routing, per-channel, and one-way-dispatch requests require a maintainer product decision on one canonical completion-audience contract before implementation.

## What This Changes

Add a deterministic framework-level control that prevents selected sub-agent completion announcements from being delivered directly to the requester channel.

## Best Possible Solution

Define one typed completion-audience policy covering at least `channel`, `parent`, and `skip`, preserve current channel delivery as the default, and thread the selected mode through every registry, nested-run, cleanup, retry, and delivery path before documenting it.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Current-main source provides a high-confidence path: normal sub-agent completion announces have no public structural skip parameter and rely on output control tokens, while the only structural bypass found is internal to ACP completion ownership.

## Solution Assessment

No, not necessarily in the exact standalone-config form proposed. Deterministic suppression is needed, but a unified typed completion-audience contract is narrower and less likely to drift than separate boolean, routing, and channel-specific controls.
