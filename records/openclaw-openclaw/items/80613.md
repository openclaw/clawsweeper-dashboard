## What This Changes

The issue reports raw managed dreaming block text leaking into promoted MEMORY.md entries and CJK snippets being deduped with ASCII-only tokenization in memory-core dreaming.

## Best Possible Solution

Land https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/pull/80620 or a narrow replacement for shared CJK-aware dreaming dedupe, then land a focused rehydration sanitizer/regression for the managed-block leak or explicitly track that remainder in a canonical memory-core issue.

Yes for the CJK dedupe path: current main’s dreaming tokenizer drops CJK-only text and falls back to exact equality. ## Solution Assessment

Yes for the CJK half: reusing or extracting the existing CJK-aware MMR tokenizer is the narrowest maintainable fix. The promotion leak should be solved at the rehydration/sanitization boundary with a regression fixture, not bundled with unrelated TTS work or a broad durable-memory policy change.
