## Summary

Keep open: current main and v2026.6.6 already queue recall tracking for `memory_search`, but the reporter’s examples are outside the dated daily-note eligibility accepted by `recordShortTermRecalls`; deciding whether broader memory paths should feed deep promotion remains an unresolved memory-core policy and live-repro question.

## What This Changes

The issue asks whether live `memory_search` should increment dreaming recall counters after deep sleep promotes no candidates and the short-term store only contains session-corpus daily entries.

## Best Possible Solution

Settle and document the promotion-source contract; if broader live-search hits should reinforce deep promotion, update path eligibility, diagnostics, docs, and regression tests in coordination with https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/issues/72021.

## Reproduction Assessment

Partly. Source inspection reproduces why the reporter's cited `MEMORY.md`, month-only, and general plan-file hits do not create recall entries, and why session-corpus-only daily entries do not promote; it does not live-reproduce a dated daily-memory hit failing to persist.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. The existing `memory_search` hook is the right boundary for eligible hits, but broadening eligible paths or changing the deep gate needs a maintainer decision on promotion-source semantics rather than a blind wiring fix.
