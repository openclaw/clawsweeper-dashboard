## Summary

Keep open. Current main has transcript-noise cleanup and role-token stopword guards, but REM and Dream Diary material selection still uses fixed corpus scores, recurrence/count-heavy themes, and candidate confidence without a semantic-importance selector; related memory-core issues overlap but do not fully replace this Dream Diary material-selection request.

## What This Changes

Request semantic-importance based Dreaming material selection so summaries surface meaningful decisions, preferences, conclusions, and unusual events instead of high-frequency transcript terms.

## Best Possible Solution

Define an internal material-importance scorer for REM and Dream Diary input, coordinate it with the broader corpus-quality and single-event promotion trackers, and avoid exposing a public promptStyle knob until the ranking contract is settled.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level: current main still builds REM themes from concept-tag counts, uses recurrence-weighted candidate confidence, and feeds those outputs into Dream Diary. The reporter's exact output is not live-reproducible without their private corpus.

## Solution Assessment

Partly. A semantic-density or material-importance selector before REM and narrative generation is the maintainable direction, but adding a public promptStyle setting first would expose product surface before the internal ranking policy is settled.
