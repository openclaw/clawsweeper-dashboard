## Summary

Keep open. Current main has useful stopword, chatter-filtering, and grounded backfill work, but live REM reflections still rank repeated concept tags and Dream Diary still consumes recurrence-shaped themes without a semantic material selector.

## What This Changes

Requests that memory-core Dreaming choose REM and Dream Diary material by semantic importance rather than repeated word or concept frequency.

## Best Possible Solution

Define an internal material-importance scorer for live REM and Dream Diary input, coordinate it with the broader memory-core corpus-quality trackers, and avoid exposing a public promptStyle knob until the ranking contract is settled.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, at source level: current main still builds REM themes from repeated concept-tag counts and feeds those themes into Dream Diary. The reporter's exact output is not live-reproducible without their private corpus.

## Solution Assessment

Partly. A semantic-density or material-importance selector before REM and narrative generation is the maintainable direction, but adding a public promptStyle setting first would expose product surface before the internal ranking policy is settled.
