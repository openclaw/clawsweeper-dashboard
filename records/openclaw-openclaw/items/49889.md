## Summary

Keep open. Current main still has internal Telegram lane outcomes for preview finalization and fallback sends, but the operator-facing hook/docs surfaces do not expose a stable delivery outcome taxonomy for sent vs preview-finalized vs preview-retained vs canceled/failed/skipped outcomes.

## What This Changes

Add clear observability for Telegram partial-stream final delivery outcomes so operators can distinguish fresh sends, preview finalization/retention, hook cancellation, transport failure, and intentional skips.

## Best Possible Solution

Implement an additive Telegram delivery-observability contract: carry a structured final outcome through lane delivery, fresh-send delivery, hook cancellation, fallback/skip, and transport-failure paths; expose it through hook/log surfaces or a companion delivery event; and document the fields for Telegram partial streaming and message hooks.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. A source-level reproduction is clear on current main: with Telegram partial streaming, final delivery can return preview-finalized or preview-retained from the lane deliverer, while hook contexts only expose success/error/messageId and cancellation simply continues without an outcome event.

## Solution Assessment

Yes. The narrow maintainable fix is additive outcome metadata or a companion delivery event; forcing fresh final messages or disabling preview finalization would change user-visible Telegram behavior instead of solving the observability gap.
