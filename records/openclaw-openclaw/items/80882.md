## Summary

Keep open: the PR targets a plausible WhatsApp idle-connection bug, but the timeout change does not line up with the current controller semantics and needs revision before merge.

## What This Changes

The PR adds a five-minute WhatsApp `unavailable` presence keepalive on open sockets and changes the auto-reply message-idle watchdog default from 30 minutes to 4 hours.

## Best Possible Solution

Land a focused WhatsApp fix that adds the periodic `unavailable` presence keepalive while preserving a maintainer-approved effective app-silence watchdog window, covered by a targeted timer test.

## Reproduction Assessment

No, not as a high-confidence current-main live reproduction. The PR body gives strong real idle-account logs from a patched install, and current source still lacks periodic presence, but the 499 portion appears partly addressed by current transport-activity watchdog behavior.

## Solution Assessment

No, not as written. The presence keepalive is a narrow owner-local fix, but changing `messageTimeoutMs` to 4 hours also expands the current controller's multiplied app-silence cap to 16 hours for quiet starts.

## Review Findings

Overall correctness: patch is incorrect

Overall confidence: 0.84

Full review comments:

- **[P2] Keep the silence watchdog at the intended window:** `extensions/whatsapp/src/auto-reply/monitor.ts:260`
  - body: Changing the default `messageTimeoutMs` to 4h also feeds `WhatsAppConnectionController`, which derives `appSilenceTimeoutMs` as `messageTimeoutMs * 4` for connections not opened after recent inbound. That makes the quiet-account app-silence safety net 16h, not the 4h described here, so a WS-alive but app-silent session can sit stale for most of a day. Please keep the effective cap at the intended window or pass a separate value for the longer app-silence guard.
  - confidence: 0.86
