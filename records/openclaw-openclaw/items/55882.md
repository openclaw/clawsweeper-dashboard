## Summary

Keep open. Current main and v2026.5.20 have adjacent pending-final, heartbeat, and commitments safeguards, but there is still no durable pre-interrupt pending-deliverable or task-stack contract; the fresh WeChat report on v2026.5.20 reinforces that the user-visible delivery/session-state gap remains.

## What This Changes

Ensure long-running agent tasks and promised user-visible deliverables survive heartbeat, system-event, or task-switch interruptions and are later delivered, deferred, cancelled, or resumed.

## Best Possible Solution

Define a typed session-continuity contract that records promised deliverables before preemptive switches and resolves them only by send, explicit deferral, cancellation, or supersession while reusing the shipped heartbeat, commitments, and pending-final safeguards.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear until maintainers define the product contract. A durable pending-deliverables stack may be the right direction, but it needs explicit cancellation, supersession, replay, and duplicate-prevention semantics before implementation.
