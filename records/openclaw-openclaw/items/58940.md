## Best Possible Solution

Use an owned process probe that preserves a wall-clock timeout, terminates or reaps the child reliably, and carries regression coverage or redacted runtime proof for a hanging `--version` fixture.

## Reproduction Assessment

No high-confidence live reproduction is present. Source inspection shows the relevant current-main timeout race and the PR's potential hang path through `execFile` timeout semantics, but no hanging Tailscale-compatible probe artifact was provided or run.

## Solution Assessment

No, not as written. The direct `runExec` await removes the outer wall-clock settlement and relies on `execFile` close after `SIGTERM`; a bounded helper that owns child cleanup is safer.
