## Summary

Keep open. Current main still exposes and consumes model-supplied exec policy controls, and #76370 is the active open fix path rather than a reason to close before merge.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to keep exec policy controls out of model-controllable tool-call args and derive them from trusted config/session policy instead.

## Best Possible Solution

Review and land #76370 or an equivalent patch that removes/ignores model-supplied exec policy controls while preserving trusted config and session-default policy paths with regression coverage.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source-level reproduction is high confidence: current main exposes the policy fields in the exec tool schema, forwards them to the model-facing OpenAI tool definition, and consumes `security`/`ask` during dispatch.

## Solution Assessment

Yes. The right fix direction is to split model-facing command/runtime args from operator/session policy and normalize or ignore model-supplied policy controls; a docs-only or error-message change would leave the boundary intact.
