## Summary

Keep open. Current main still exposes and consumes model-supplied exec policy controls, and the linked open PR is the active fix path rather than a reason to close before merge.

## What This Changes

Remove or ignore model-supplied exec policy controls so `security`, `ask`, `host`, `elevated`, and `node` come from operator/session policy rather than tool-call arguments.

## Best Possible Solution

Review and land #76370 or an equivalent patch that splits model-visible exec args from operator policy, preserves trusted config/session override paths, and adds schema plus dispatch regression coverage with docs/changelog follow-through.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes, source-level reproduction is high confidence: on current main, a tool call can provide `security: "deny"` or `security: "allowlist"`, those args feed the effective exec policy, and the host approval path emits the same denial family described in the report.

## Solution Assessment

Yes, the best direction is to split model-facing command/runtime arguments from operator/session policy and normalize at dispatch; a docs-only or error-message change would leave model-controllable policy intact.
