## Summary

Keep open. Current main still exposes and consumes model-supplied exec policy controls, and the linked open PR #76370 is the active fix path rather than a reason to close before merge.

## What This Changes

The issue reports that `exec` exposes operator-only policy controls (`security`, `ask`, `host`, `elevated`, `node`) in the model-facing tool schema and then honors model-supplied values.

## Best Possible Solution

Review and land #76370 or an equivalent patch that removes or ignores model-supplied exec policy controls while preserving trusted config and session-default policy paths with regression coverage.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source inspection on current main shows a model/tool call that includes `security: "deny"` can flow through `createExecTool`, become effective exec policy, and reach the reported denial error.

## Solution Assessment

Yes. Splitting model-facing command/runtime args from operator/session policy is the narrow maintainable fix; a docs-only change would leave the boundary intact.
