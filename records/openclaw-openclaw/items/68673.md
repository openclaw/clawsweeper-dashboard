## Best Possible Solution

Keep the `update_plan`-specific normalization, but retain a stable material step-content signal while ignoring cosmetic explanation or echo churn, then cover both collision and progress cases with tests and a changelog entry.

Yes. A source-level reproduction is clear: record 30 `update_plan` calls whose non-empty step text changes while statuses and plan length stay fixed, then call `detectToolCallLoop`; the PR head hashes those calls as the same no-progress signature.

No. Normalizing `update_plan` args and outcomes is the right narrow area, but dropping step content is too coarse; canonicalized step text or per-step content hashes would avoid cosmetic churn without colliding distinct plans.
