## Summary

Keep open: current main still has ACP availability, allowlist, thread-bound spawn controls, and announcement delivery retries, but it does not have a general per-channel ACP deny/warn policy or same-shape completion-event coalescing for noisy child sessions. The related execution-backend issue is adjacent and explicitly treats this as a separate policy/rate-limit concern rather than superseding it.

## What This Changes

The issue requests a channel-aware ACP spawn policy and/or completion coalescing to prevent ACP child-session failure floods from non-interactive channels such as Telegram DM.

## Best Possible Solution

Define a small channel-aware ACP safety contract, then implement the chosen guard and any announcement coalescing with focused coverage for `sessions_spawn(runtime="acp")`, ACP permission-denial failures, and Telegram topic/DM routing.

## Reproduction Assessment

No high-confidence live reproduction was established. Source inspection does confirm current main lacks the requested channel-wide ACP spawn policy and same-shape completion coalescer, and docs confirm the non-interactive ACP permission failure mode.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear as proposed. A per-channel deny/warn policy is plausible, but maintainers should choose whether the durable fix belongs in spawn policy, announcement coalescing, ACP permission-denial retry handling, or a combination.
