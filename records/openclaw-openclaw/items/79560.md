## Summary

Keep open: current main still has ACP availability, allowlist, inherited-tool, sandbox, concurrency, and thread-bound spawn controls, but it does not have the requested channel-wide ACP deny/warn policy or same-child/same-shape completion-event coalescing for runaway permission-denial loops. Related ACP permission and stall issues are adjacent but do not supersede this flood-control request.

## What This Changes

Request a channel-aware ACP spawn policy and/or announcement coalescing to prevent ACP child permission-denial loops from flooding non-interactive channels such as Telegram DM.

## Best Possible Solution

Define a small channel-aware ACP safety contract, decide whether it should be paired with identical completion-event coalescing or ACPX retry bounds, then document and test the chosen behavior across `sessions_spawn(runtime="acp")`, Telegram DM/topic routing, and announcement delivery.

## Reproduction Assessment

No high-confidence live reproduction was established. Source and docs do confirm the relevant non-interactive ACP path and the absence of a general per-channel ACP spawn guard or same-shape completion coalescer on current main.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear as proposed: per-channel deny/warn is plausible, but maintainers should choose whether the durable fix belongs in channel spawn policy, announcement coalescing, ACPX permission-denial retry handling, or a combination.
