## Summary

Keep open: current main has global ACP gates, target allowlists, max-concurrency controls, and thread-bound spawn controls, but it does not have the requested per-channel ACP deny/warn policy or identical completion-event coalescing. The remaining work is real, but it needs maintainer product/API judgment because the issue proposes three possible mitigation layers.

## What This Changes

Request a channel-aware ACP spawn safety policy and/or announcement coalescing to prevent non-interactive channels such as Telegram DM from flooding chats with repeated failed ACP background-task announcements.

## Best Possible Solution

Define a small channel-aware ACP safety contract and the chosen announcement dampening behavior, then cover `sessions_spawn(runtime="acp")` from Telegram DM/topic and ACP task terminal delivery with focused tests.

## Reproduction Assessment

No high-confidence live reproduction was established. Source inspection confirms the relevant non-interactive ACP path, background-task completion delivery path, and absence of a channel-level ACP spawn guard or same-shape completion coalescer on current main.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear as proposed: per-channel deny/warn is plausible, but maintainers should choose whether the durable fix belongs in channel policy, task-announcement coalescing, acpx retry handling, or a combination before implementation.
