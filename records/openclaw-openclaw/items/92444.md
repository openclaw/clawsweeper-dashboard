## Label Justifications

- `P2`: A healthy provider can remain unusable for up to five hours after credits return, but impact is bounded to affected profiles and fallback models may remain available.
- `impact:auth-provider`: Persisted billing-disable state prevents provider selection despite restored upstream availability.

## Summary

Current main and v2026.6.6 retain the reported deadlock: billing disables are explicitly excluded from the existing throttled recovery probe, so a restored profile cannot reach the success reset until its persisted window expires. Related open PRs shorten cooldowns or clear state after reauthentication, but none clearly owns recovery immediately after credits are restored.

## What This Changes

Add a supported early recovery path for billing-disabled provider profiles after credits are restored.

## Best Possible Solution

Add one canonical billing-recovery contract at the model-fallback/auth-state boundary: either a conservatively throttled half-open attempt that re-arms the disable on failure, or a doctor/CLI reset that validates the credential live before clearing the actual agent store; cover fresh exhaustion, restored credits, restart persistence, and concurrent attempts.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Current-main source and tests establish the path: billing creates a five-hour disabled window, preflight selection suspends billing profiles without probing, and the success reset cannot run without dispatch.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. Reusing the existing half-open seam is the narrowest automatic fix, but maintainers must choose its billing cadence and concurrency contract; a live-validated operator reset is the safer explicit alternative.
