## Summary

Current main and release v2026.6.5 still limit pairing to direct messages; group admission remains open, disabled, or allowlist-based, so the requested owner-controlled group workflow remains unimplemented and needs product and security design before coding.

## What This Changes

Add owner-approved group admission, group-join notifications, approve/reject operations, and optional restricted observation for groups awaiting approval.

## Best Possible Solution

Establish an additive generic group-admission lifecycle with account-scoped pending state, authenticated owner notifications, and authorized approve/reject operations exposed through plugin-safe channel hooks; treat silent observation or reader-agent processing as a separately reviewed mode unless maintainers deliberately include it in that security contract.

## Reproduction Assessment

Not applicable; this is a request for a new group admission and notification workflow, and current source and documentation consistently show that no such contract exists.

## Solution Assessment

No, not as one combined change: approval, notification, rejection, and restricted observation have distinct ownership and security concerns. The best path is to design the generic group-admission lifecycle first and decide separately whether reader-only processing belongs in it.

## Risks / Open Questions

- A group approval store must be keyed and authorized correctly across channel, account, and group identities to avoid cross-account approval leakage.
- Owner notifications and inline approvals could expose group metadata or permit unauthorized approval actions unless recipient and command-owner checks are explicit.
- Routing hostile pending-group content into any agent, even a nominally read-only one, introduces prompt-injection and session-association questions that are separate from admission control.
- Combining Telegram and WhatsApp behavior without a generic lifecycle contract could produce channel-specific state and inconsistent rejection or leave behavior.
