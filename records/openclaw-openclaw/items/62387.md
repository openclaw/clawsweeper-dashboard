## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` still has the reported source path: for named-account channel configs without `namedAccountPromotionKeys`, promotion selects policy/default keys, moves them into `accounts.default`, and removes them from the channel root, while Slack and Discord named accounts only inherit those defaults from the root.

## What This Changes

The issue asks OpenClaw to preserve shared channel defaults during multi-account promotion for channels that do not declare `namedAccountPromotionKeys`.

## Best Possible Solution

Preserve root policy and delivery defaults for channels whose named accounts inherit from the channel root, while keeping explicit `accounts.default` shared-default behavior for channels that intentionally support it, with focused Slack/Discord, Telegram, Matrix, WhatsApp, and Google Chat regressions.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. Source inspection gives a high-confidence path: a Slack or Discord config with named accounts and root `allowFrom` is selected for promotion, deleted from the root, and then no longer inherited by the named account according to current account-resolution tests and docs.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear. The narrow bug is real, but the best fix needs a maintainer decision between per-channel `namedAccountPromotionKeys`, a safer generic fallback, or an explicit shared-default-account opt-in for channels that use `accounts.default` as inherited state.
