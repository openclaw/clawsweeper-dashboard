## Best Possible Solution

Land a revised cron-service fix that rechecks deletion after reservation but before delivery side effects for timed and queued manual runs, treats store reload failures as per-job outcomes, and keeps focused regression coverage.

Yes. A high-confidence source-level reproduction is to collect or queue a cron job, delete the persisted job before the worker starts, and observe current main executing the captured job snapshot without a pre-execution store recheck.

No. The PR direction is the narrow maintainable fix, but the timer existence check must be handled inside the per-job outcome path so one store reload failure cannot abort the whole timer tick.
