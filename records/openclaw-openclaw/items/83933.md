Yes. Source inspection shows the PR preserves manual result state, then the post-run maintenance recompute can treat the due slot as already executed and clear `nextRunAtMs` for a successful `at` job.

## Solution Assessment

No. The `applyJobResult` discriminator is the right layer, but the current branch does not preserve that invariant through post-run recompute; the safer fix is to skip the just-manual job or teach expired-slot recompute to ignore manual terminal state.
