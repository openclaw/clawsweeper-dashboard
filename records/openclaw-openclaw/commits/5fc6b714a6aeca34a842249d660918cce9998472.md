Found one low-severity QA reliability regression in the WhatsApp live transport readiness wait. The Matrix timeout/prompt changes are internally consistent and the focused Matrix scenario tests pass.

## Findings

### Low: WhatsApp stability wait can fail late-starting channels that previously passed

- Kind: reliability
- File: `extensions/qa-lab/src/live-transports/whatsapp/whatsapp-live.runtime.ts`
- Line: 439
- Evidence: `waitForWhatsAppChannelStable` now uses one `WHATSAPP_QA_READY_TIMEOUT_MS` wall-clock loop, but each iteration calls `waitForWhatsAppChannelRunning`, which has its own full 150s wait at `extensions/qa-lab/src/live-transports/whatsapp/whatsapp-live.runtime.ts:388`. If the channel first becomes connected near the end of that inner wait, the stability helper sleeps for the remaining 20s stability window at lines 449-450, then exits the outer loop and throws at line 453. The parent commit’s implementation waited for readiness, slept, then performed a second independent readiness wait, so a slow but successful startup could still pass.
- Impact: WhatsApp live QA can incorrectly fail with `did not remain ready for 20000ms` when startup takes close to 150s, even if the account stays connected after it finally becomes ready. This is the opposite direction of a commit intended to extend stability windows.
- Suggested fix: Separate the first-ready deadline from the stability deadline. For example, wait once for ready, then require `lastConnectedAt + WHATSAPP_QA_READY_STABILITY_MS` to be reached with a follow-up status check, allowing at least the stability window after first readiness.
- Confidence: high

## Details

- Do we have a high-confidence way to reproduce the issue? yes; use a fake gateway where `channels.status` reports the WhatsApp account disconnected until just before `WHATSAPP_QA_READY_TIMEOUT_MS`, then connected with `lastConnectedAt` set to that late timestamp. The current helper will sleep for the stability window and then throw after the outer deadline has expired.
- Is this the best way to solve the issue? yes; the stability window should be measured after the account first becomes ready, while still rechecking status to catch disconnect/reconnect.
